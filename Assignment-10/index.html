Helpful Definitions

Medium
The binder used to create your paint such as oil, gum, egg, acrylic, etc.
Vehicle
Thinner in a paint such as solvent, water, or alcohol.
Dispersion
The pigment in a vehicle alone with no binder.
Paint
The pigment and binder in a final mix.

How to Make Medium

Medium: Step 1

  1. Measure 240 ml (8 fluid ounces or 1 cup) of oil.

Medium: Step 2

  1. Measure 28g (1 ounce) of white beeswax.

Medium: Step 3

  1. Combine oil and beeswax into heating unit.

Medium: Step 4

  1. Measure out 720 ml (24 fluid ounces or 3 cups) of additional oil and put it in a glass container that can hold over a quart of finished medium.

Medium: Step 5

  1. Add warmed medium to glass container where you put the new oil.

Medium: Step 6

  1. Shake or stir to combine finished medium.

How to Make Your Own Oil Paint

Paint: Step 1

  1. Place measured pigment on glass slab.

Paint: Step 2

  1. Indent the pile of pigment in order to make a well.
  2. Add a little bit of medium to the pigment.

Paint: Step 3

  1. Mix the medium and pigment with a palette or putty knife, collecting it into a pile.

Paint: Step 4

  1. Begin mulling as soon as enough medium has been incorporated.
  2. Place your hand on the neck of the muller close to the base.
  3. Begin mulling in a circular motion, making the circle larger and larger across the slab.
  4. Mull sufficiently before deciding to add more of the vehicle or medium.
  5. Your mixture may appear too dry at first but will liquify with continued mulling.
  6. This step could take anywhere from 10-20 minutes depending on the pigment.

Paint: Step 5

  1. Prepare your pastry bag so you can fill it by yourself.
  2. Label your empty paint tube or glass jar

Paint: Step 6

  1. Fill the pastry bag with paint

Paint: Step 7

  1. Fill the metal tube, starting towards the bottom. Make sure you tap the tube on the table as you go so the paint goes to the end of the tube.
  2. Make sure you only fill the tube 2/3 of the way. You don't want to over fill it.

Paint: Step 8

  1. Crimp the end of the tube with the toothpaste squeezer.
  2. Fold the crimped end over on itself.

Paint: Step 9

  1. Put a small dab of the finished paint to the outside of the label so you know what is looks like.
  2. Set the tube aside where the label won't get smudged.
