Scary Stories to Tell in the Dark

Written by Alvin Schwartz
Illustrated by Stephen Gammell

Alvin Schwartz compiled the short stories for these books through use of American folklore. Schwartz collected these stories and turned them into tales of horror and revenge guaranteed to “chill your bones".

According to the American Library Association, the series was the single most banned and challenged books in the United States. This was a result of many people believing the content and illustrations to be of “inappropriate” content for children. Between the gruesome stories and graphic surrealist style illustrations, many adults believed it would traumatize a whole generation of children.

Although many of the stories seemed unforgettable in and of themselves, the drawings were quite scary and seem to have a lasting impression on the readers themselves. In 2011, for the 30th anniversary of Scary Stories to Tell in the Dark, the books were rereleased with different illustrations from Brett Helquist. Many original fans of the books complained about the new version of the illustrations being “watered down and not that scary anymore”. This outcry caused the books to be rereleased with original artwork by Stephen Gammell in 2017.
